# Discovery Questions Bank

Full question bank for each of the five lenses.
Read during Step 2 of the product-soul workflow when you need more questions for a specific lens.
Stop each lens the moment you have enough to write that section — do not run through the full list by default.

---

## Lens 1 — User

**Must answer (stop here if these are clear):**
- Who is the primary user? Describe them as a specific person in a specific situation — not a demographic or persona archetype.
- What are they doing today instead of using this product, and what is painful or costly about that?
- In one sentence, what would they say this product does for them?

**Go deeper if the user picture is unclear:**
- When do they encounter the problem this product solves? (morning, quarterly, during a specific workflow?)
- What have they tried before? Why did those solutions fall short?
- What would have to be true for them to pay for this?
- Who else is affected when they use this product? (colleagues, customers, family?)
- What would they tell a friend this product is for?

---

## Lens 2 — Business

**Must answer:**
- How does this product make money? (subscription, transaction fee, usage-based, freemium-to-paid, services attached, etc.)
- What does the business need to be true in year 1 to justify continuing?
- What does the business need to be true in year 3 to call this a real company?
- What is the single biggest business risk right now?

**Go deeper if the model is unclear:**
- What is the unit economics hypothesis — directional CAC vs LTV?
- Is there a revenue model that the competition has proven works in this space?
- What does "healthy" look like at 100 customers? 1,000?
- Who controls the budget that pays for this? (end user, their manager, IT, procurement?)
- Are there regulatory, compliance, or platform dependency risks that affect the business model?

---

## Lens 3 — Strategy

**Must answer:**
- Name 2–3 alternatives a user would genuinely consider. (Specific product names, not categories.)
- For each: why would a user choose this product instead?
- What is the one capability or insight this product has that competitors cannot easily replicate?
- What is the strategic bet — the assumption about the market or user that must be true to win?

**Go deeper if strategy is vague:**
- Is the moat technical, network-based, data-based, or distribution-based?
- What would a well-funded competitor need to invest to catch up?
- Is this a "better product" play or a "different wedge" play?
- What does the competitive landscape look like in 3 years if this succeeds?
- What are the most dangerous adjacent competitors — ones not in the space yet but could enter?

---

## Lens 4 — Product-Market Fit

**Must answer:**
- Has this product been used by real users in a real context yet? (Not a demo, not a friend.)
- What happened? What was the retention / return behaviour?
- What specific behaviour would constitute PMF signal — not a vanity metric?
- What would make you confident PMF is NOT there?

**Go deeper if PMF picture is unclear:**
- What is the "aha moment" — the first time a user gets genuine value?
- How long does it take from signup to aha moment?
- Are there users who would be "very disappointed" if the product disappeared? How many? (Sean Ellis test)
- Is there a specific segment where retention is clearly better than others?
- What do churned users say when they leave?

**PMF signal frameworks to apply:**
- **Retention curves:** Do they flatten at >30 days, or drop to zero?
- **Sean Ellis test:** % of users who would be "very disappointed" if product disappeared — >40% is strong signal
- **NPS:** Is it positive, and do promoters specifically mention the core value prop?
- **Word-of-mouth:** Is any acquisition happening without intentional marketing?

---

## Lens 5 — GTM Distribution

**Must answer:**
- How does the very first user find this product? (Specific channel and trigger, not "through marketing".)
- What is the wedge distribution channel — the one that works before you have scale?
- What is the acquisition → activation → retention loop?

**Go deeper if GTM is unclear:**
- What is the activation moment — the first time a user genuinely gets value, not just completes signup?
- What is the time-to-activation today? What would great look like?
- Is the GTM motion self-serve, sales-assisted, or community-led? Which should it be at this stage?
- What is the referral or expansion mechanism — how does one user become two?
- For the first 100 users: what is the exact tactic, not the strategy?
- Is there a content or SEO surface that creates organic inbound?
- Is there a community or ecosystem this product plugs into?

---

## When to Stop

Stop each lens when you can write a specific, honest sentence for that section.
Do not continue asking questions if:
- The user is speculating without data — capture the hypothesis and move on
- You have enough to write a non-vague, non-aspirational sentence
- Additional answers would only add detail, not change the strategic picture
